**Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. 
Instead of hacking systems, criminals exploit human trust and lack of awareness.

A typical case begins when a victim receives a call, SMS, or message on apps like WhatsApp or Google Pay. 
The fraudster pretends to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer/seller. 
Step 1: They create urgency—claiming a refund, prize, or issue with the user’s account. 
Step 2: They send a “collect request” (payment request) via a UPI app. 
Step 3: The victim is instructed to “approve” the request by entering their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving money. 
Step 4: Once the PIN is entered, money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

Targets are usually students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payments. 
Small business owners and online sellers are also common victims due to frequent transactions.

The consequences can be serious. 
Victims may lose significant amounts of money within seconds, often with little chance of recovery due to the instant nature of UPI transfers. 
It can also lead to stress, loss of trust in digital systems, and financial instability.

This case highlights the importance of awareness: never enter your UPI PIN to receive money, verify unknown contacts, and avoid clicking suspicious links.
